Why this website?

Built to make deepfake review feel practical

This project is designed to look and behave like a real investigation workspace. The goal is not just to label content, but to present the reasoning, the history, and the evidence in a way that feels usable.

The challenge

Manipulated media and misleading claims appear across social, messaging, and the open web, so a single-signal detector is rarely enough.

Our approach

A hybrid pipeline analyzes text and URLs, then blends local model output with explanation and evidence layers.

Transparency

Labels, confidence, and history are visible by design so the project feels accountable and easier to trust.

Unified workflow

One place to screen news text and URLs with consistent outputs and a unified review flow.

Calibrated confidence

Scores are constrained to avoid overconfidence in borderline cases and make the UI easier to interpret.

Privacy-first

Your predictions are scoped to your account; delete or export anytime.

Good product feel

Typography, spacing, and history panels are tuned so the project reads like a finished system rather than a prototype.
